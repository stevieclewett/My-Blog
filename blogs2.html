Why Accounting!? Blog

This blog post is not intended to talk about my regret of choosing accounting as a major but...

I’m 99% sure that if Professor Reynolds had not been my professor,I would not have graduated as an accounting major. I’m not sure what I would have done though...maybe teaching or management? Now don’t get me wrong, I, in no way, regret my decision to go into accounting. It is a valuable skill set to have and I have already seen the benefits of majoring in accounting. The part of accounting I struggle with most is the lack of human interaction I get. I know some accountants have the luxury of working with/getting new clients but I haven’t been able to see that side of accounting yet.

If I could go back and change my decision to major in accounting, would I? Not a chance. I tell myself that I worked too hard for this damn degree and I am SO close to finishing my masters, so I can’t be regretful of any decisions I have made that got me to where I am now. I know at the end of the day, those degrees mean something. I also do my best to remember how fortunate I am to have the opportunity to be getting my masters degree in accounting. There are so many people who would kill to have the opportunities that I have right now in my life. So for that, I am incredibly grateful.

I also appreciate being able to read a financial statements and have an understanding of how most companies operate from an internal perspective. I have the knowledge and skills of an accountant (which is awesome), but not the personality. For this reason, I feel stuck. I think what I am missing from my current job though is the face to face interactions and meeting new people. I will say though, I have been extremely lucky to work where I do now and start my professional career off in such a positive way. The partners, managers, and coworkers I work with now are truly good people. I know that everyone I work with genuinely wants the best for me, even if that means I have to leave Tyler to work somewhere else.

I am looking for a job that will allow me to use my accounting degree, or business knowledge in general. Honestly though, I don’t really know what that looks like yet. I have been searching for new jobs and I think I have an idea of what I would like to do, but I also think that no one really knows what they want to do until they have actually tried it.

So for now, I am keeping my options open and prayers up until I find where I am supposed to be.
